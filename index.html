How Our Digital Logic Tools Help Students and Engineers

1. Visual Circuit Simulator: This tool allows users to interactively build and simulate complex digital logic circuits using a drag-and-drop interface. It supports a wide array of logic gates, flip-flops, multiplexers, and ALU components. It helps students understand real-time signal propagation, combinational logic, and sequential logic without requiring expensive hardware setups.

2. FSM Designer & Generator: The Finite State Machine designer provides a graphical environment to draw state diagrams (Moore and Mealy machines). It helps users transition from conceptual state diagrams to physical digital hardware by automatically generating synthesizable Verilog HDL code, making it an invaluable tool for VLSI and FPGA design courses.

3. Logic Quest Puzzle Game: Designed to reinforce theoretical Boolean concepts, this interactive puzzle game challenges users to build specific truth tables using a restricted inventory of logic gates. It acts as an engaging educational gamification tool, helping beginners master universal gates (like NAND and NOR) and logic minimization techniques.

4. Interactive K-Map Solver: Our Karnaugh Map tool simplifies complex Boolean algebra expressions visually. It supports up to 6 variables, automatically finding the prime implicants and the simplified Sum of Products (SOP) expression. This saves engineering students hours of manual computation and helps them intuitively grasp digital circuit minimization.

About SQGATE

Free online logic gate simulator — design, simulate, and export Verilog in your browser.

What is SQGATE?
SQGATE is a powerful, client-side digital logic circuit simulator designed explicitly for computer science and electrical engineering students. Whether you are learning the basics of Boolean algebra or designing a complex 16-bit RISC-V CPU, SQGATE provides a frictionless, zero-latency environment to test your architectures.

Why use SQGATE?
Unlike traditional legacy EDA tools that require heavy desktop installations, SQGATE runs entirely in your web browser. This means you can build logic circuits on your laptop, tablet, or even your smartphone. Our proprietary simulation engine resolves gate propagations in real-time, allowing you to instantly visualize state changes with glowing wires and interactive toggle switches.

Advanced Features for Students
We go far beyond basic AND, OR, and NOT gates. SQGATE includes advanced components like D-Flip Flops, multiplexers, and 7-segment displays. Furthermore, our platform is actively expanding to include a Truth Table Generator, a Karnaugh Map (K-Map) Solver, and an integrated Verilog Simulator. You will soon be able to directly export your visual circuits into clean, synthesizable Verilog HDL code, perfectly bridging the gap between schematic design and hardware description languages.

Privacy-First Architecture
We believe student data should remain private. All of your circuit designs and project files are saved locally in your browser's memory. We do not require a login, and we do not store your designs on our servers, ensuring absolute privacy for your intellectual property.

Digital Logic Simulator Guide

Welcome to the SQGate digital logic simulator. This comprehensive educational tool allows you to design, build, and test complex digital circuits directly in your web browser without any installation required.

Whether you are an engineering student learning the fundamentals of Boolean algebra, or a hobbyist prototyping a complex CPU architecture, our intuitive drag-and-drop interface provides a seamless workflow. Start by selecting basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, XOR, NAND, NOR) from the Library panel on the left, and drag them onto the infinite grid canvas.

Connect the terminals using the intuitive wiring system. Simply click on an output pin and drag to an input pin. SQGate automatically routes the wires, but you can add waypoints by clicking on the canvas during routing to keep your schematics organized. To test your logic, use the Toggle Switches and Push Buttons to inject high (1) or low (0) signals into your circuit, and monitor the outputs using LEDs or the Seven-Segment Display.

Advanced Features: Sequential Logic and Waveforms

Unlike simple combinational logic simulators, SQGate fully supports sequential logic elements. You can incorporate D Flip-Flops, T Flip-Flops, JK Flip-Flops, SR Latches, and specialized memory modules (RAM/ROM) into your designs. Use the Clock component to drive synchronous circuits like counters and shift registers.

For rigorous timing analysis, open the Waveform Viewer from the top navigation bar. By clicking 'Record', the simulator captures the exact timing diagrams of every named node in your circuit. This is invaluable for debugging race conditions, setup/hold time violations, and analyzing propagation delays across complex data paths.

Exporting to Hardware Description Languages (HDL)

SQGate bridges the gap between graphical schematic capture and actual hardware synthesis. Once your design is verified, you can instantly export it to industry-standard Verilog or VHDL code using the 'Code' button. This generated HDL can then be synthesized using tools like Vivado or Quartus and deployed directly onto an FPGA.

If you want to create your own custom components, simply select a cluster of gates and click Create IC. This packages your logic into a single reusable integrated circuit module that you can drop into any future project. All projects are saved locally in your browser, or can be downloaded as a `.sqg` file to share with classmates or colleagues.

Browser-Based Logic Gate Simulator

Welcome to the ultimate logic gate simulator. Whether you are a student learning Boolean algebra or a professional prototyping a microprocessor, our logic simulator provides an infinite canvas with real-time evaluation.

Interactive Digital Design

Our simulator features a comprehensive components library including basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR), complex combinational circuits (Multiplexers, Decoders, ALUs), and sequential logic elements (D Flip-Flops, J-K Flip-Flops, Counters, Registers). You can easily drag and drop components, wire them up, and use our interactive inputs (switches, buttons, clocks) to observe the digital states instantly.

Advanced Hardware Prototyping

Beyond simple circuits, our tool supports advanced hierarchical design (custom ICs), multi-bit buses, waveform timing analysis, and even structural Verilog HDL export. This makes it an invaluable resource for computer architecture courses, GATE exam preparation, and bridging the gap between schematic capture and FPGA/ASIC synthesis.
