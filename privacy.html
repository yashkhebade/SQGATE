Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 2024

Welcome to SQGATE. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your data when you use our educational digital logic and circuit simulator. Because SQGATE is built with a privacy-first, client-side architecture, our policy is extremely simple: we do not collect, store, or transmit your circuit designs or personal data to any external servers.

1. Data Storage and Processing

SQGATE operates entirely within your web browser. All processing, simulation, and logic calculations are performed locally on your device (Client-Side).

  • Project Data: All circuit designs, configurations, and state history are saved using your browser's local storage mechanisms (such as localStorage).
  • No Cloud Backend: We do not operate any databases or servers that receive your designs. If you clear your browser cache or site data, your projects will be permanently deleted unless you have exported them as JSON files.

2. Third-Party Analytics

We may use basic, anonymized website analytics (such as Google Analytics or Umami) strictly to understand aggregate site traffic (e.g., how many visitors use the site and which pages they visit). These tools may use cookies, but they do not capture the contents of your circuits or personally identifiable information.

3. External Links

Our platform contains links to external sites, such as GitHub and Ko-Fi. Once you click these links and leave SQGATE, you are subject to the privacy policies of those respective platforms.

4. Children's Privacy

Because SQGATE is an educational tool that does not collect personal data, it is safe for students and users of all ages to use without requiring parental consent for data collection.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time if we introduce new features (such as optional cloud saving). Any updates will be posted on this page.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please reach out by opening an issue on our GitHub Repository.
