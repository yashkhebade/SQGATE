Combinational Logic: Adders & Math

June 30, 2026 • 6 min read

We know logic gates process bits, but how do computers actually do math? The answer lies in Combinational Logic, specifically Adder circuits.

The Half Adder

To add two 1-bit numbers, we need a circuit that outputs a Sum and a Carry. If you add 1 + 1 in binary, the result is 10 (0 for the Sum, 1 for the Carry).

  • Sum: The XOR gate perfectly models the Sum. 1 + 0 = 1, but 1 + 1 = 0.
  • Carry: The AND gate perfectly models the Carry. It only outputs a 1 if both inputs are 1.

The Full Adder

A Half Adder is great, but what if you need to add multi-bit numbers? You need to account for a "Carry In" from the previous column. This is where the Full Adder comes in. It takes 3 inputs (A, B, Carry-In) and outputs Sum and Carry-Out.
