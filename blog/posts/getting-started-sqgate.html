Getting Started with SQGATE: The Ultimate Logic Simulator

June 29, 2026 • 5 min read

Welcome to SQGATE! If you're studying digital electronics, computer architecture, or just looking to build some cool hardware logic, you're in the right place. SQGATE is a lightning-fast, entirely web-based logic gate simulator that lets you design, test, and export circuits without ever downloading heavy desktop software.

1. The Workspace Overview

When you launch a free workspace, you are greeted with a clean, dark-themed infinite canvas. On your left is the Component Library, containing everything from basic logic gates (AND, OR, XOR) to advanced sequential components like D-Flip Flops, Registers, and RAM.

To add a component, simply drag it from the library onto the canvas. You can click on any component to reveal its properties in the right-hand panel—allowing you to adjust bit-widths, toggle states, or rename labels.

2. Wiring It Up

Connecting components in SQGATE is intuitive. Just click on an output node of one component, and drag the wire to an input node of another. The Liquid Glass UI engine visualizes active signals instantly: a glowing cyan wire means the signal is HIGH (1), while a dark blue wire means the signal is LOW (0).

3. Exporting to Verilog

One of SQGATE's most powerful features is its real-time HDL translation. Once your circuit is behaving as expected, click the Verilog button in the top navigation bar. SQGATE will instantly generate clean, synthesizable Verilog code that maps perfectly to your schematic.

module circuit (
  input A,
  input B,
  output OUT
);
  assign OUT = A & B;
endmodule

4. Next Steps

Now that you know the basics, try building a simple Half Adder! If you want to dive deeper, check out our premium tools like the 6-Variable K-Map Solver and the FSM Designer.

Happy designing!
